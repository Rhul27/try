Our Team

Our Team

Welders

Welders are skilled workers who join or fuse metal parts together using various welding techniques, such as arc welding, MIG welding, or TIG welding. They play a crucial role in the fabrication and assembly of crane components.

Turner

Turners, also known as lathe operators, operate lathe machines to shape and cut metal parts with precision. They work with rotating metal pieces and use cutting tools to create cylindrical or conical shapes required for crane components.

fitter

Fitters are responsible for assembling and fitting various crane parts together. They ensure that the components are aligned, secured, and functioning correctly. Fitters often use hand tools, power tools, and measuring instruments to perform their tasks.

Electricians

Electricians specialize in electrical systems and play a vital role in the installation, wiring, and maintenance of electrical components in cranes. They ensure that the electrical systems are functioning properly and meet safety standards.

Fabricators

Fabricators work with metal sheets and structural components to create the required shapes and structures for cranes. They may use cutting, bending, and shaping techniques to fabricate metal parts based on engineering drawings or specifications.

Mechanic

Mechanics in this context would be responsible for maintaining and repairing the mechanical components of the cranes. They have expertise in diagnosing and troubleshooting mechanical issues and performing necessary repairs or replacements.

Painters

Painters are involved in the finishing process of the crane components. They apply paint or protective coatings to the metal surfaces to enhance their appearance and protect against corrosion.

Polishing and Buffing Workers

These workers are responsible for the final polishing and buffing of crane parts. They use specialized tools and techniques to achieve a smooth and polished surface finish.
